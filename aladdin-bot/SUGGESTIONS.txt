ALADDIN BOT V7 — 100 IMPROVEMENT SUGGESTIONS
======================================================================
Based on full codebase audit. Each item is a concrete, implementable
suggestion with a clear reason and expected benefit.
======================================================================


STRATEGY & SIGNAL GENERATION (20 items)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

1.  Add a Tokyo session strategy (similar to LondonOpenStrategy) that
    trades the JPY open at 00:00 UTC. Currently the bot has no Asian-
    specific logic — Tokyo accounts for ~5% of daily forex volume.

2.  Add a breakout-fade strategy: when price breaks a key level but
    volume does NOT confirm, fade the move. The existing BreakoutStrategy
    only trades confirmed breakouts, missing the majority of false
    breakouts that are tradeable in the opposite direction.

3.  Add a news-driven mean-reversion strategy: after a high-impact
    news event, price often overshoots and reverts. Enter 15-30 min
    after the event (once the blackout lifts) in the reversion direction.

4.  Add a currency strength matrix that ranks all 8 major currencies
    (USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, AUD, CAD, CHF, NZD) individually by their
    average strength against all peers. Trade the strongest vs weakest
    pair rather than only pre-configured pairs.

5.  Add a carry trade strategy layer: automatically identify pairs where
    interest rate differential > 2% (from swap rates already in
    TRADING_CONFIG.swapCosts) and size up long positions in high-carry
    pairs during low-volatility regimes.

6.  Add an Asian range breakout strategy: track the USDJPY/AUDUSD high
    and low from 00:00–08:00 UTC (Asian range), then trade the breakout
    when London opens at 08:00. A well-known institutional pattern.

7.  Add divergence detection: when price makes a new high but RSI makes
    a lower high (bearish divergence), or price makes a new low but RSI
    makes a higher low (bullish divergence). Currently no strategy uses
    divergence signals.

8.  Add a momentum reversal detector: 3 consecutive same-direction
    closes with declining volume typically signals exhaustion. Enters
    counter-trend once the volume decline is confirmed.

9.  Add calendar spread awareness: track the spread between near/far
    futures contracts (from COT data already being fetched). Extreme
    contango or backwardation in FX futures often precedes directional
    moves.

10. Add a multi-pair basket strategy: when EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and
    AUD/USD all show BUY signals simultaneously, enter all three with
    1/3 normal size each. The correlation reduces single-pair risk while
    capturing the USD weakness theme.

11. Add a volatility breakout strategy using Bollinger Band squeeze:
    when BB width compresses to its lowest 10-percentile over 100 bars,
    set an alert for an imminent expansion. Enter in the direction of the
    first 2-bar close outside the band.

12. Add overnight gap fade strategy: measure the gap between Friday's
    close and Sunday/Monday's open. Gaps > 0.3% tend to fill within the
    first session — fade the gap direction with tight stops.

13. Add a support/resistance level mapper that identifies historical
    price clusters with high touch-count (> 3 touches). Block entries
    within 5 pips of a major level; instead trade the bounce or break.

14. Add a higher timeframe confluence filter: before any H1 entry, check
    that the daily (D1) candle body trend agrees. Currently RegimeStack
    uses D1 data but does not enforce D1 trend alignment as a hard gate.

15. Add a fair value gap (FVG) / imbalance detector: when a candle's
    low is above the previous candle's high (bullish FVG), mark the zone
    as a buy area. Institutional traders actively fill these gaps.

16. Add a spread-normalised momentum indicator: divide the price rate-of-
    change by the current bid-ask spread. This identifies momentum that
    is large relative to trading costs, filtering out moves too small to
    profit from after spread.

17. Add a time-of-day performance heatmap that weights entries by their
    historical profitability per 30-minute UTC window. Currently
    TimeHeatmap exists but is stored by hour — 30-min granularity is
    more precise for intraday forex.

18. Add a pairs correlation divergence strategy: when two normally
    correlated pairs (e.g. EUR/USD and GBP/USD, r > 0.85) diverge by
    > 1.5 standard deviations, trade the convergence. Separate from the
    existing correlation engine which only blocks correlated entries.

19. Add an order block detector: identify the last up/down candle before
    a strong impulsive move (institutional order blocks). These zones
    act as future support/resistance with measurable edge.

20. Add an economic surprise model: compare actual data releases vs
    analyst consensus (from a free calendar API). Positive/negative
    surprises have directional predictive power for 30-60 minutes.


RISK MANAGEMENT (15 items)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

21. Add a portfolio heat limit: cap the total concurrent risk across ALL
    open positions at 3% of equity. Currently SectorCap limits sector
    exposure but there is no global "portfolio heat" ceiling.

22. Add a maximum daily trade count limit (e.g. 10 per day). Over-
    trading is a known path to ruin — busy days with 20+ signals often
    produce worse outcomes than filtered 5-signal days.

23. Add a losing streak scaling: after 3 consecutive losses, reduce
    position size to 50% until a winning trade occurs. After 5 losses,
    reduce to 25%. Currently consecutive-loss circuit breaker only halts,
    it doesn't gracefully scale down.

24. Add a Calmar ratio monitor: if the trailing 12-month Calmar ratio
    (annual return / max drawdown) drops below 0.5, switch to a
    conservative-only mode that blocks mean-reversion trades and only
    takes high-confidence trend signals.

25. Add a volatility-adjusted stop loss: when ATR is 2× its 20-bar
    average (volatility spike), widen stops proportionally rather than
    using the fixed ATR multiplier. Tight stops in high-vol regimes
    produce unnecessary stop-outs.

26. Add a margin utilisation monitor: calculate current margin used as
    a percentage of account equity. Block new positions when margin
    utilisation > 20% to preserve buffer for adverse moves.

27. Add a max hold time rule: automatically exit any position held > 48
    hours that has not hit stop or take-profit. Stale positions decay
    in edge and accumulate overnight swap costs.

28. Add a news-aware stop tightening: 5 minutes before a scheduled
    HIGH-impact event, tighten the stop on any open position to 50% of
    normal ATR multiple to protect against gap risk at the event.

29. Add a position age decay: as a position ages beyond 4 hours without
    hitting its target, gradually reduce take-profit distance and tighten
    trailing stop. Edge decays over time.

30. Add a cross-asset crisis detector: if VIX (or XAU/USD spike > 2%
    in 1 hour) signals a risk-off episode, immediately reduce all
    position sizes by 50% and widen stops. The leading-indicator fetcher
    already has DXY/XAU but doesn't use them for crisis detection.

31. Add a weekend risk rule: automatically reduce position size to 25%
    for any trade opened after 21:00 UTC Friday. Weekend gaps are
    unhedgeable and the reduced size reflects the overnight risk premium.

32. Add a slippage budget per session: track cumulative slippage in pips
    per day. If slippage exceeds 5 pips in a session, switch from market
    to limit orders only for the remainder of the day.

33. Add a correlation clustering: group open positions into clusters by
    their rolling correlation. If any cluster has > 3 positions with
    r > 0.7, block new entries to that cluster entirely, not just cap
    the exposure size.

34. Add a realised vs expected volatility divergence alert: when 1-hour
    realised vol is more than 2× the daily ATR-implied vol, send a
    Telegram alert and skip new entries until vol normalises. Indicates
    a fat-tail event in progress.

35. Add a drawdown-velocity alert: if equity drops more than 1% in any
    30-minute window (regardless of the 48h weekly limit), send an
    immediate alert and pause new entries for 1 hour.


EXECUTION ENGINE (10 items)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

36. Add OCO (One-Cancels-Other) order support: submit stop-loss and
    take-profit as a linked pair so that when one fills, the other is
    automatically cancelled. Currently both are managed manually in
    exitPosition, creating a window where both could trigger.

37. Add a limit order retry mechanism: if a limit order is not filled
    within configurable timeMs, automatically re-price to market bid/ask
    rather than cancelling. Reduces missed fills in fast markets.

38. Add partial position scaling: enter at 50% of target size on initial
    signal, then add the remaining 50% if price moves in the intended
    direction by 0.5×ATR within 30 minutes. Pyramiding into winners.

39. Add a spread spike detector in the execution path: if spread widens
    > 3× normal in the 100ms before order submission (detected from
    bid/ask history), delay the order by 1 bar. Wide-spread entries
    dramatically reduce EV.

40. Add execution cost pre-trade calculation: before entering, compute
    expected round-trip cost (spread + commission + estimated slippage)
    as a fraction of the expected move. If cost > 30% of expected move,
    block the entry.

41. Add an order replay log: persist every order submission, fill,
    cancel, and amend to a separate JSONL file (separate from audit log).
    This enables post-trade execution quality analysis without polluting
    the main audit log.

42. Add support for trailing stop orders on the broker side (not just
    software): submit native OANDA trailing stop orders when available.
    Server-side trailing stops survive disconnections; the current
    software trail in exitPosition does not.

43. Add a fill quality alert: if 3 consecutive orders have slippage > 2
    pips, send a Telegram alert and switch to limit orders. ExecutionMetrics
    already measures slippage but there is no alert trigger on a streak.

44. Add an iceberg order mode for large positions: when positionSize >
    2× the average trade value, split into 3 iceberg slices with random
    30–90 second intervals between each slice to avoid market impact.

45. Add a minimum holding period: block exit signals within the first
    5 minutes of entering a position. Prevents stop-hunting noise from
    triggering premature exits on the same bar as entry.


DATA & MARKET INTELLIGENCE (15 items)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

46. Add a real news sentiment API integration: replace the synthetic
    NewsFilter event list with a live feed from NewsAPI.org or similar
    (free tier available). Currently all news events are manually seeded
    or approximated.

47. Add Fear & Greed Index polling: fetch the CNN Fear & Greed Index
    daily (free, public endpoint). When Fear & Greed < 25 (extreme fear),
    reduce position sizes by 30%. When > 75 (extreme greed), apply the
    same reduction.

48. Add real-time economic calendar from ForexFactory or Investing.com:
    scrape the free weekly calendar rather than maintaining a static
    FOMC/ECB schedule. Events shift dates quarterly and the current
    approximation will drift.

49. Add options market implied volatility: fetch USD/EUR 1-week implied
    vol from CME or Yahoo Finance (free). When IV > 20%, widen all stops
    by 25%. When IV < 8%, tighten them by 15%.

50. Add a retail sentiment indicator from broker COT reports: OANDA and
    IG publish real-time retail long/short ratios for free. These are
    strong contrarian signals — when > 75% of retail are long, bias
    toward short.

51. Add a G10 rate differential tracker: maintain a table of central
    bank policy rates and compute the rate differential for each traded
    pair. High differential = carry trade tailwind; include it as a
    feature in the ML model.

52. Add a geopolitical risk score: subscribe to a free geopolitical risk
    index (e.g. GPRC from Fed Atlanta) or compute a proxy from news
    headline sentiment. Reduce risk exposure when geopolitical risk
    index > historical 90th percentile.

53. Add GDP surprise and PMI data: track the monthly GDP and PMI releases
    for USD/EUR/GBP/JPY. Persistent above-consensus PMI (3+ months) is a
    strong directional driver that complements the existing economic calendar.

54. Add a cross-market signal from equity index futures: when S&P 500
    futures are down > 1% pre-open, reduce USD long exposure (risk-off
    correlation). The leading-indicator fetcher has XAU but not SPX.

55. Add Bitcoin as a leading risk indicator: BTC/USD has shown strong
    correlation with risk-on/risk-off episodes. A BTC > 3% drop within
    1 hour often precedes USD strength and AUD/NZD weakness.

56. Add a yield curve slope monitor: track the US 2Y-10Y spread (free
    from FRED API). Yield curve inversion historically precedes USD
    weakness 6-12 months later — use as a medium-term regime bias input.

57. Add tick data aggregation for better VWAP: the current VWAP
    estimation uses M5 close prices. Accumulating actual tick volume per
    bar would give a more accurate VWAP, improving entry quality.

58. Add a real-time swap/funding rate feed: OANDA provides live swap
    rates via API. Use these to dynamically adjust the carry component
    in price-divergence.js rather than the static TRADING_CONFIG.swapCosts
    table.

59. Add seasonal pattern detection: some pairs have statistically
    significant seasonal tendencies (e.g. USD tends to strengthen in
    September). Include a monthly seasonal bias factor that slightly
    tilts Kelly sizing in the seasonal direction.

60. Add a central bank meeting calendar countdown: beyond the existing
    blackout, track the number of trading days until the next central
    bank decision and gradually reduce position sizes as the date
    approaches (the "pre-FOMC drift" reduces edge 2 days before).


BACKTESTING & VALIDATION (10 items)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

61. Add a transaction cost sensitivity analysis: re-run every backtest
    at 1×, 1.5×, and 2× current commission/slippage assumptions and
    report which strategies remain profitable. Strategies that break
    even at 1.5× cost are fragile.

62. Add a regime-conditional backtest: explicitly test each strategy
    only on data classified as its target regime. TrendStrategy should
    only be benchmarked on TRENDING periods, not RANGING ones.

63. Add a live-vs-backtest performance tracker: maintain a running
    comparison of live trading performance vs the most recent walk-
    forward estimate. Alert when live Sharpe deviates > 1σ from
    backtest expectation.

64. Add a multi-currency backtest: test all 8 major pairs simultaneously
    under the same parameter set. A strategy that only works on EUR/USD
    but fails on GBP/USD is likely curve-fitted to EUR/USD microstructure.

65. Add a tick-level replay mode: instead of M5 OHLCV candles, replay
    backtest using M1 candles to simulate more realistic intrabar
    execution. Avoids look-ahead bias in stop and limit fills.

66. Add a stress test module: replay the backtest over known historical
    crisis periods (2008 GFC, 2015 CHF unpeg, 2020 COVID crash, 2022
    USD rally) and report maximum drawdown. The Monte Carlo exists but
    doesn't use real crisis data.

67. Add a benchmark comparison: compute the bot's Sharpe, Calmar, and
    total return vs a simple buy-and-hold EUR/USD strategy and a moving-
    average crossover system. Without a benchmark, outperformance cannot
    be assessed.

68. Add a bootstrap confidence interval for strategy metrics: report
    the 5th–95th percentile range for Sharpe ratio and max drawdown
    from the block bootstrap, not just point estimates.

69. Add a forward test lock: once a parameter set passes walk-forward,
    lock it for a minimum 30-day forward test at paper trading before
    applying to live. Currently auto-grid can push params to live within
    minutes of a passing backtest.

70. Add slippage impact modelling in backtests: currently backtest uses
    a fixed slippage fraction. Model it as a function of position size
    relative to average volume — large-for-the-market orders face
    proportionally higher slippage.


MONITORING & OBSERVABILITY (10 items)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

71. Add a Grafana-compatible metrics export: extend the Prometheus
    endpoint to include trade-level histograms (fill latency, slippage,
    win rate by hour) so operators can build real-time dashboards.

72. Add a P&L attribution breakdown: report separately how much profit
    came from each strategy, session, and currency pair. Currently the
    audit log has the raw data but no aggregation tool exists.

73. Add a daily performance email/webhook report: send a structured daily
    summary (trades, P&L, drawdown, open positions) via email or Discord
    webhook at 00:00 UTC. Currently only Telegram alerts on events.

74. Add a system resource monitor: track CPU%, memory MB, and Node.js
    event loop lag. Alert if event loop lag > 100ms (tick processing
    backlog) or memory > 400MB (possible memory leak).

75. Add a data feed health dashboard: show per-source latency (OANDA,
    Alpha Vantage, COT) and freshness. Currently price-divergence.js
    tracks staleness but there is no aggregated view.

76. Add an anomaly detector for trade metrics: using a rolling z-score,
    alert when any metric (average fill latency, slippage, win rate)
    deviates > 2σ from its 30-day rolling mean. Catches execution
    degradation before it becomes visible in P&L.

77. Add a position reconciliation report: every hour, log a reconciliation
    between what the local state says is open and what OANDA's API shows.
    Any mismatch triggers an immediate alert. Uses the existing
    OandaReadonlyClient.

78. Add a kill-switch via Telegram command: allow the operator to send
    `/halt` or `/resume` to the Telegram bot and have the engine respond.
    Currently halts are only triggered by automated rules, not manually.

79. Add structured logging with log levels (DEBUG/INFO/WARN/ERROR):
    the current `this.log()` is a flat console.log. Structured logs
    (JSON per line with level, module, timestamp) enable log aggregation
    tools like Datadog or ELK.

80. Add a weekly performance review auto-report: every Monday at 09:00
    UTC, generate and email a PDF/HTML report summarising the past week:
    P&L, drawdown, trade count, best/worst trade, regime breakdown.


DEPLOYMENT & OPERATIONS (10 items)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

81. Add a blue/green deployment mode: run a new bot version in shadow
    mode (taking signals but not executing) alongside the live bot for
    48 hours before switching. Prevents regressions from reaching live
    capital.

82. Add a Kubernetes/Helm chart: extend the existing Docker setup with
    a Helm chart for Kubernetes deployment. Enables auto-scaling of
    non-trading workers (backtest runners) and better secret management
    via K8s Secrets.

83. Add a database migration system: as the schema evolves, add a
    migrations/ folder with numbered SQL files. Currently the DB is
    rebuilt from scratch on schema changes, losing historical data.

84. Add a secrets manager integration (AWS Secrets Manager or HashiCorp
    Vault): currently secrets are in .env files. On a production server,
    secrets should be fetched at startup from a vault, not stored on disk.

85. Add a canary release system for strategy parameters: after auto-grid
    finds new params, apply them to 10% of capital for 24 hours. If
    performance is within expectation, promote to 100%. Prevents a bad
    parameter set from immediately impacting full capital.

86. Add automated infrastructure monitoring with alerts: monitor that
    the PM2 process, health endpoint, and OANDA connectivity are all up.
    If any check fails for > 2 minutes, send a PagerDuty/Telegram alert.

87. Add a daily .env integrity check: at startup, verify that all
    required environment variables are set AND that the API keys are
    valid by making a test request to each API. Fail fast with a clear
    error rather than discovering a bad key mid-trade.

88. Add a trading session scheduler: allow configuring specific hours
    when the bot is active per day (e.g. only London and NY sessions).
    Currently the bot either runs 24/7 or not at all.

89. Add a multi-account support mode: allow the bot to manage multiple
    OANDA sub-accounts simultaneously (e.g. aggressive and conservative
    risk profiles on separate accounts).

90. Add an audit log shipping to cloud storage: periodically upload
    trade_logs/ to an S3-compatible bucket (MinIO for self-hosted).
    Local disk failure should not result in lost trade history.


MACHINE LEARNING & ADVANCED FEATURES (10 items)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

91. Add online learning for the confidence calibrator: currently Platt
    scaling parameters are fit at startup. Implement streaming updates
    that adjust calibration after each closed trade, keeping calibration
    accurate as market conditions drift.

92. Add a transformer-based sequence model for regime detection: replace
    or augment the ADX-based regime classifier with a small transformer
    trained on OHLCV sequences. Even a 2-layer model with 16 hidden units
    can capture non-linear regime transitions.

93. Add feature importance tracking: after each closed trade, record
    which indicator features (RSI, MACD, ADX, session, etc.) had the
    highest influence on the decision. Accumulate these over time to
    identify which features are becoming less predictive.

94. Add Bayesian parameter updating: instead of periodic grid search,
    update strategy parameters continuously using a Bayesian posterior
    from closed trade outcomes. The Bayesian optimizer exists but is not
    connected to the live parameter update loop.

95. Add a market regime forecast horizon: the current regime stack
    classifies the CURRENT regime. Add a forecaster that estimates the
    PROBABILITY of regime change in the next 4 hours, allowing
    pre-emptive size reduction before a regime transition.

96. Add an adversarial robustness test: deliberately inject noisy or
    shifted input data during backtesting and measure how much strategy
    performance degrades. Strategies that are robust to input noise are
    less likely to fail on live data quality issues.

97. Add a neural network-based price impact model: given the current
    order size and market depth, predict expected slippage. Train on
    historical fills stored in the execution log. Use this to adjust
    Kelly sizing downward for large orders.

98. Add a multi-agent strategy tournament: run 5-10 strategy variants
    in a simulated tournament (similar to A/B testing but multi-armed)
    and continuously allocate capital to the current best performer via
    a multi-armed bandit algorithm.

99. Add transfer learning from related instruments: pre-train the ML
    model on liquid instruments (EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY) and fine-tune
    on less-liquid pairs (NZD/USD, USD/CAD). This addresses the sparse
    data problem for exotic pairs.

100. Add a model explainability layer (SHAP values): for every BUY/SELL
     decision above 80% confidence, compute SHAP values showing which
     input features drove the signal. Log these and surface them in the
     dashboard. Operators should be able to understand why the bot
     entered a trade.
